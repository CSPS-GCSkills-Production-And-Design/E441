To: Lise Binette
From: Antonio Pellegrini
Subject: Mentoring Program for New Recruits

Greetings Lise,

To answer your question, I would say that mentoring is an effective way to facilitate the career development of recruits. When recruits have the chance to find a good mentor, they have ready access to plenty of information about the organization, and get the opportunity to find out about work strategies that would otherwise take them a lot of time to discover. Mentoring somewhat accelerates the integration of new recruits into the corporate culture of the department. I’m no expert, but I tend to believe that recruits who have good mentors feel that they have access to more resources and thus have more confidence and perform better at work. Obviously this is only true insofar as the mentor is a good match with the mentee. It is not always easy to find the right combination of mentor and protégé. Obviously, some matches are more effective than others.

That’s what I think in a nutshell. I wish you good luck in setting up your program. I am sure it will be of great value to a number of recruits.

Antonio feels that a mentee needs self-confidence to find a good mentor.
Antonio believes that it is important to find a good mentor-mentee pairing.
