To: Jafar
From: Christine
Subject: Response to your Mini Survey

Dear Jafar,

Your survey was very interesting. Personally, I would say that the ability to communicate effectively is the most important skill a manager can have. What I think is that even if managers are good planners who can easily manage a budget, resolve problems or negotiate agreements, if they cannot communicate, they cannot really lead because they would have no way to convey their thoughts and expectations.

Regarding the question as to whether it is better to learn to manage through training or through having a variety of experiences and positions, I would think that real-world experience counts much more than training. In my opinion, there are certain delicate situations that managers have to deal with that no amount of instruction can prepare them for. Without being an expert, I am of the opinion that living through these experiences is the best way of learning to manage them. Having gone through these experiences, we learn more from life lessons than we do from training.  

I hope that my standpoint on this is useful to you.
