Read the text and indicate if the statements that follow are true or false.

SOCIAL ACTIVITIES DURING WORK HOURS

Some question the conduct of social activities in government offices.

Such activities give the impression that employees are wasting their time, but is this really the case? First, in reality, there are very few of these activities, and if you take the trouble to examine the impacts of such activities, you will find that they are positive. What’s important here is the choice of activities.

Through social activities, internal communication becomes more efficient, team spirit improves and the sense of belonging gets stronger. As we know, when a group of people collaborate and work together closely, the performance of the group is vastly improved, which results in saving both time and money.

Knowing that these activities have a positive effect on both motivation and the quality of work, why do employers not use them more to acknowledge the work done by the employees? Especially since, as demonstrated in several studies, financial rewards are very ineffective at boosting performance.

There is, therefore, no reason to believe that these activities interfere with the proper performance of employees. It is truly unfortunate that the public continues to believe that social activities at work are a waste of time and that employees abuse these situations.

Answer choices:
According to the author, people who collaborate are more effective at work.
The author believes that time is money.
The author feels that employers should give more cash rewards to highly effective employees.
From the author’s perspective, social activities can have a positive impact on the public’s perception of the public service.
