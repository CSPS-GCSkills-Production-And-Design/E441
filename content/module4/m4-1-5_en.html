Table of expressions 2

Verb tenses in conditional clauses
Real situation
{If + present} + present If we give you an assignment, you do it.
{If + present} + future If we give you an assignment, you will do it.
{If + present} + imperative If we give you an assignment, do it.
Unreal situation
{If + past} + past modal*
*(would/could/might/should)		 If we gave you an assignment, you would do it.
If + past perfect + perfect modal*
*(would/could/might/should + have + past participle)		 If we had given you an assignment, you would have done it.

Reminder:

In present unreal conditionals, "were" is used for all persons in the if-clause.

Generally the future or past modals are not used in the if-clause except when we are making a very polite request or in cases where if means "whether."
