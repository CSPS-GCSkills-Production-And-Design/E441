What is GBA+ for Status of Women Canada

Gender-Based Analysis PLUS (GBA+) is a method used to study how sex and gender intersect with other identity factors (age, language, sexual orientation, education, ability, geography, culture, ethnicity, income). When applied to government work, GBA+ can help us understand how diverse groups of women and men experience public policy in Canada. The factors that are important depend on the situation. 

Key GBA+ questions

Taking gender into consideration can be as easy as asking the following questions: Does an issue affect women and men in different ways? If so, how? Are specific groups more adversely affected by the issue? Does the initiative or proposed solution improve the situation for all? Or does it have an uneven effect or create barriers for some?

GBA+ example 

For example, for the anti-smoking campaign, GBA+ could be used to address questions such as the following: Given that people have very different reasons for smoking, how do we create an effective anti-smoking campaign? To answer this question, one should consider the gender component. Young women and men cite different reasons for smoking. Girls cite curiosity, making friends and fitting in whereas boys cite pleasure, looking tough and being independent. There are other factors to take into consideration as well: low levels of education, lone parent status and lack of social support also play a role in smoking behaviour. The combination of these and other factors, along with gender, should therefore be considered when designing an effective anti-smoking campaign.
