A CAREER FAIR

During a job fair, a young graduate came to the booth and said he thought it was almost impossible these days to pass a job interview if you are not already in the federal public service. He also mentioned that he had just had an unsuccessful interview. He did not know what to do to improve his performance, and he was a bit demoralized.

He was told that the interview is a key component of any job search and that to boost his chances of success, he should have visited the department’s website to find information on its mission, its mandate, its programs and its services. He should also have analysed the requirements of the position in order to anticipate questions that might be asked and prepare answers.

Furthermore, he should have thought about his skills, his limitations, his strengths and his weaknesses, and about what qualified him for that job. It would have been a good idea to think about the kind of person he is so as to be able to describe himself.

Finally, he could have thought about questions he wanted to ask during the interview. The members of the selection board would then have realized that he was prepared.

At the booth, he asked a number of questions and took note of the answers. At the end of the conversation, he seemed to be reassured and confident.

Adapted from material on careers in the federal public service at the website of the Public Service Commission of Canada
