Activity 1.1

Read the text and indicate whether the statements that follow it are true or false.

To: Wallace MacMillan
From: Emma Johnson
Subject: Open House

Dear Mr. MacMillan,

I would be happy to meet with you to discuss the arrangements for your Open House event. Meanwhile, here are some answers to the questions you asked in your e-mail.

Regarding the types of activities, I think they should be brief and interactive. If you could think of some possible activities, we could discuss them at our meeting, and I could give you an opinion on how successful they would likely be in the context of an Open House.

You also wondered whether it is worth distributing promotional items. While it is important that visitors leave with something that relates to your institution, promotional objects are often expensive. Since your budget is rather limited, I would opt for pamphlets. They are less costly and they have the added advantage of providing information. We can of course discuss this further during our meeting.

Finally, have you thought of any particular theme for your Open House? Selecting a theme helps focus your message, stimulates creativity and generally has a positive impact on visitors. I would encourage you to think about this before our meeting.

I have attached my schedule. Please tell me a time and place that would be convenient for you.

Answer choices:
Emma is answering questions ahead of time because she’s afraid she won’t have enough time during the meeting.
Emma would like to discuss handing out pamphlets during the event because she is not sure it is a good idea.
Emma thinks that the activities put on for the visitors should allow them to take an active part in the Open House.
Emma thinks Mr. MacMillan should make a greater effort to have interesting activities at the Open House.
