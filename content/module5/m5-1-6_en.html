Example

Listen to the example, paying attention to the expressions used to convey cause and effect.

For more than fifty years, various levels of government have been encouraging Canadians to lead healthy lives. Having been told to eat well, be active and manage stress, Canadians as a whole could be expected to be in exceptional health. However, that is not the case.

Because an increasingly fast lifestyle imposes career, family and other obligations on us, we eat fast food, which makes us gain weight. Since we no longer have the time to exercise regularly, we do not reach our goals in this area either. Finally, the guilt of not having the time to do everything we should be doing to be healthy raises our stress level so that, here again, we fail.
