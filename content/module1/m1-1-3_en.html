Strategies

Some strategies to consider

  • Make sure that you fully understand what the speaker has said before you respond.
  • Use the right linking words to transition from one item of comparison to another.
    Example: This criterion is more important than the previous one; however, it is used less often.
  • Include adverbs to qualify the comparisons. Example: This computer is noticeably faster than the other one.
  • Refer to another person or group of people to introduce the comparison. Examples: Scientists have proven . . ., people tend to think . . ., studies have shown . . . .
