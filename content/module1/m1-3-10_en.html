Since we can’t get together, here is a brief overview of how I expect the workshop to go.

First, we can divide the group of participants into sub-groups and ask them to discuss their ideas on how to make a work team more effective. Then, in a plenary discussion, they will present the ideas they discussed in their sub-groups. Finally, using a visual presentation, we can review the important aspects, such as the roles and responsibilities of the leader and members of an effective team and the differences between an effective work team and an ineffective work team.

Here is some information on the differences between an effective work team and an ineffective work team. In order to help you prepare the presentation, I found this information in a document that the Department has used in previous workshops.

On an effective team, members trust one another and participation is encouraged, whereas there is a lack of trust among members of an ineffective team, which breeds mistrust and creates conflicts. Leadership is shared on an effective team and objectives are clear. On an ineffective team, the roles and responsibilities are not clear, and objectives and tasks are not well defined. Finally, effective teams develop and discuss ideas as a group, and all team members agree on decisions, whereas on an ineffective work team, ideas are neither analysed nor developed, and often, topics other than the matter at hand are discussed.

Feel free to make changes to the text if you wish. What’s important is that you identify what makes a work team effective and what makes it ineffective.

I hope this information will help you prepare the workshop.
